The brief

Your team has a once-in-a-career opportunity

Rumour has it our Managing Director has a secret fund. A large, glorious, sun-drenched fund earmarked for team holidays — flights, hotels, food, the lot. No expense limits. No questions asked.

There is just one condition: whoever wants access to this fund must submit a formal proposal via a GitHub Pull Request. That’s right. The MD is a person of culture. He only accepts proposals that have been version-controlled, peer-reviewed, and merged into the main branch.

If your proposal gets approved and merged, the holidays are yours. If it gets rejected with a comment saying “needs more evidence” — back to the office it is.

This workshop is your preparation. By the end, your team will know exactly how to use GitHub to collaborate, propose, and — most importantly — get those holidays approved.

Skills you’ll gain

What you’ll learn today

Six things standing between you and that all-inclusive resort. Each one a skill. Each skill a step closer to the MD’s approval.

Module 01

Git fundamentals

Repos, commits, branches. The basics that separate those who get holidays from those who still share files over email — in 2026.

Module 02

Branching & merging

Work in parallel without stepping on each other’s toes. Your proposal needs multiple contributors — branches make that possible without chaos.

Module 03

Pull requests

The golden ticket. This is how you submit your holiday proposal. Learn to open a PR so well-structured the MD simply cannot say no.

Module 04

Code review

Peer review your proposal before the MD does. Comment. Suggest. Improve. Catch the weak arguments before they get rejected with extreme prejudice.

Module 05

Collaboration workflow

Issues, assignments, milestones. Organise your team so everyone knows who’s drafting “budget justification” and who handles “risk mitigation.”

Module 06

Best practices

Commit messages, README files, branch naming conventions. Yes, the MD will check. He has surprisingly strong opinions about meaningful commit messages.

How it works

Five steps to holiday approval

01

Learn the tools

Walk through Git and GitHub basics. No experience needed. We start from zero — because even the most senior person here probably has confused ideas about rebasing.

02

Form your team

Assemble your crew. Every team needs a strategist, a writer, someone good with spreadsheets, and at least one person who knows what “git stash” does.

03

Draft the proposal

Structure a compelling case. Destination, budget, ROI for the company (yes, you need that bit), and why your team deserves this more than anyone else.

04

Collaborate on GitHub

Branch, commit, review, and iterate. Use everything you’ve just learned. This is the real exercise — the proposal is just a delicious excuse.

05

Submit the PR

Open your Pull Request. Write a clear description. Assign reviewers. Address feedback with dignity. Then wait nervously for the MD’s verdict.

Workshop plan

Today’s schedule

Expand each block to see what’s covered. The MD will not be in the room during the session — but he will be reading your PRs after.

Theory + hands-on

What we cover: What is version control and why it matters. Git vs GitHub. Creating your first repository. Understanding the commit history.

We’ll walk through the interface together and make sure everyone can clone a repo, create a file, and commit a change before we move on. No one gets left behind — this is a team sport.

Hands-on exercise

What we cover: Creating and switching branches. Merging changes back. Handling merge conflicts — which will happen, because two people will inevitably edit the same line.

We’ll simulate a real conflict and resolve it together. Think of it as a metaphor for the heated debate your team will have about whether Bali or the Maldives is the better holiday destination.

The main event

What we cover: Opening a Pull Request. Writing a good PR description. Reviewing someone else’s changes. Leaving useful comments (not just “LGTM”). Requesting changes. Approving and merging.

This is the skill that will directly determine whether you get the holidays. A well-written PR is an art. A badly written one ends up in the “Closed — won’t review” pile.

Team collaboration

What we cover: Each team works on their holiday proposal using everything they’ve learned. Assign roles, create branches, write content, review each other’s work, and submit a Pull Request.

The facilitator plays the role of the MD — reviewing your PRs, leaving comments, requesting changes, and ultimately deciding which team has earned the holiday. May the best proposal win.

Wrap-up

What we cover: Review what worked. Discuss best practices that emerged. Talk about how these skills apply in real projects — beyond the (very worthy) goal of obtaining free holidays.

Also: the MD’s verdict will be announced. Bring your best negotiating face — or just a really good proposal.
